Pork Cut Chart

A pig can be cut into more than 230 products

Jowl

Jowl, also known as "Golden 300 grams", is renowned for its even distribution of fat and lean meat, a tender texture, and marbled fat. It is suitable for grilling, deep-frying, and hot pot among other things.

Ear

Ribs

Ribs are strip-shaped and easy to make. They are rich in calcium and good for your bones. Try using them in soup, deep-frying them, or even steaming them.

Blade Shoulder

There are only 3-3.5 kgs of blade shoulder per pig. Tender, juicy, and delicious, it is best suited to char siu or pork chops among others.

Belly

Alternating layers of fat and lean meat from the belly and rib area of the pig are ideal for dishes such as Dongpo Pork, Crispy Pork Bites, and Twice-cooked Pork.

Picnic Shoulder

The picnic shoulder consists primarily of smaller pieces of lean meat with connective tissue. As pigs tend to extensively use their front legs, the texture of the meat is soft and delicious and good for home cooking and stuffed buns or dumplings, to name a few.

Front Hocks

The muscle in the hock area is mostly shank meat with a significant amount of connective tissue. After being cooked, it has a crispy texture and chewy feel. It is suitable for marinating, braising, etc.

Head

"Ingot" Meat

A muscle located in front of the pig's hind leg bone. It is oval in shape and tender in texture with crisscrossing meat fibers and less connective tissue. It is suitable for slicing and shredding for homemade dishes.

Back Trotters

The meat from the rear trotters has more bones and is straight in shape. It is rich in collagen and suited to marinating, stewing, braising, and so forth.

Loin

Loin is also known as pork tenderloin. The fat content of loin is low, making it an ideal choice for the health conscious. Good for slicing and shredding to add to homemade dishes.

Ham

Ham contains less fat and connective tissue. As pigs use their hind legs less, the meat is tenderer, making it an ideal ingredient for processed meat products.
